        <h2>About Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam</h2>
        <p>
            Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, known as the "Missile Man of India," was a visionary scientist, a great teacher, and the 11th President of India. Born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, he dedicated his life to advancing India's space and defense technology. 
        </p>
        <p>
            As a scientist, Dr. Kalam played a pivotal role in India's civilian space program and military missile development. He was instrumental in the development of the Agni and Prithvi missiles, earning him his iconic title. 
        </p>
        <hr>
        <h2>Legacy</h2>
        <p>
            Dr. Kalam was not just a scientist but also a motivational figure. His books, such as *Wings of Fire* and *Ignited Minds*, continue to inspire millions of young Indians to dream big and work hard to achieve their goals.
        </p>
        <p>
            Dr. Kalam passed away on July 27, 2015, while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management Shillong. His life remains a beacon of hope, dedication, and innovation for generations to come.
        </p>
